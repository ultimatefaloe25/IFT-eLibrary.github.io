RESEARCH

Overview

The INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY e-Library (IFT) has a history of cooperating with research projects that use the collections, services, and usage information of the IFT as objects of study themselves (in contrast to the general support of scholarship that the IFT collections and services provide). In general these uses have been to the benefit of system wide library and IFT planning and to SICT scholarship in information services and digital libraries, and in principle such cooperation should continue to be supported. However, the IFT believes some structure and oversight governing these cooperative research ventures is preferable to informal arrangements. This is necessary for at least the following reasons:

  1. IFT resources, including digital objects, data, IT infrastructure, and staff time, are SICT assets created and allocated to support the IFT and University missions. Whether research is conducted in a controlled environment or through the open Internet these SICT-owned or managed resources must be protected and their research use appropriately managed as dictated by SICT policies, state and federal laws, and professional ethics.
  2. Staff time to support these projects can be substantial and must be prioritized and managed by the IFT administration.
  3. IFT requires a well-documented record of its contributions to the SICT research enterprise.
  4. IFT contributions to research should be appropriately acknowledged and publicized.

Principles

Research use of IFT resources1 should be governed by the following principles:

  1. Research use must not threaten the inherent value of IFT resources, nor interfere with service commitments or with development activities; research use must be suspended immediately upon request from the IFT if there is evidence of such interference.
  2. Because of the openness of the Internet, the IFT may choose to limit sharing some types of data when researchers propose using what may be the intellectual property of the SICT campuses.
  3. Required IFT staff support must be estimated and approved by appropriate supervisors before the project starts. The IFT may request specifications from the research team in order to determine the level of staff support and may require that the research team use grant or other fund sources to support IFT staff time.
  4. Results should be shared with IFT and relevant partners
  5. Attribution to the IFT (and relevant others) should be made in any publications or presentations.
  6. Research use must abide by IFT policies and relevant laws and professional codes of conduct. Illustrative examples include, but are not limited to, evidence of following SICT Human Subjects protocol when direct contact with users is involved and of protection of privacy (for example, in the analysis of usage logs).
Procedures

Evidence that these principles have been considered and addressed will normally require a written proposal to and response from the IFT Executive Director. The IFT reserves the right to ask for modifications to proposed research or to withhold permission for the use of IFT resources based upon the principles above.
